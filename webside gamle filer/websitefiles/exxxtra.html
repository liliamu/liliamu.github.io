• The installation has a familiarity in seeing oneself at the other side of the tunnel, where the real-time effect acts as an immediate confirmation that the viewer’s movement creates changes in the visualisation. Yet there is an uncanniness in looking at oneself in a distorted machine vision “mirror”, merged in an almost-familiar texture, as nature seen via a machine. This parallel draws the viewer to interact with the installation by playing, exploring, and testing a threshold of the familiar and the uncanny.

    • The creative production consisted of developing, designing, and implementing the intallation. Underlying system for visualisation is an OpenFrameworks implimtation of Logan Engstroms Fast Style Transfer in TensorFlow . The installation was exhibited in collaboration with CATCH (Center for Art + Tech) , for Click festival, and showcased from May 20th to 21st, 2017, in Kulturværftet, Helsingør (pictured).
    • Also exhibited at:
    • Opening of Danish Center for Applied AI, Alexandra Institute 27 /10, 2017
    • Culture Night, IntermediaLab, ITU, 13/10, 2017
    • Convolutional neural networks were trained on the Abacus supercomputer via DeIC grant as pilot project for ITU, "Computational Creativity and Optimizing for Aesthetics"
